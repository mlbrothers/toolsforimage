Introduction

TIFF (Tagged Image File Format) is a versatile image format often used for high-quality graphics, such as photographs and scans, supporting multiple layers and color depths. It is commonly favored in professional settings for its lossless compression and ability to preserve detail. PNG (Portable Network Graphics), on the other hand, is a web-friendly format known for its support of transparency and lossless compression. While PNG is ideal for digital use, such as online images and graphics, TIFF is preferred for archiving and printing due to its superior quality.
