Introduction

TIFF, which stands for Tagged Image File Format, is a high-quality image format used by photographers and graphic designers. The meaning and use case of TIFF typically relate to preserving image data without losing quality. This makes it ideal for print media, professional photography, and when you need large, uncompressed files for high-quality outputs. On the other hand, JPG, or JPEG (Joint Photographic Experts Group), is one of the most common image formats. The meaning of JPG revolve around file compression. JPG images are smaller in size because they are compressed, making them perfect for websites, social media, and any situation where file size matters more than retaining every detail of the image.

However, there are times when you might need to convert between these formats, especially when working with different devices or platforms. For example, you may need to convert TIFF to JPG for web use because JPG images load faster due to their smaller size.
