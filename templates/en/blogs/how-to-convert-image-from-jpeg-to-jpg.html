Introduction

JPEG (Joint Photographic Experts Group) and JPG are both image file formats commonly used for storing digital photos. The primary difference between JPEG and JPG is the number of characters in the file extension: JPEG has four characters, while JPG has three. This difference originated from early Windows systems, which required three-letter extensions, hence JPG was used. Functionally, they are identical, both using lossy compression to reduce file size while maintaining image quality. Whether it's JPEG or JPG, the formats are interchangeable, and most devices and software recognize and support both without issues.
