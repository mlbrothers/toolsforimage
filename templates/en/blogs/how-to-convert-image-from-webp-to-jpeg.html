For users looking to convert WebP to JPEG, understanding the technical aspects behind the conversion process can be beneficial. WebP is a newer image format that uses both lossy and lossless compression to reduce file sizes, making it popular for web usage. However, JPEG, which also uses lossy compression, is more universally compatible, and converting between these formats involves careful handling of the image data to avoid quality loss.

When you convert WebP to JPEG, the internal process involves decoding the WebP image format into raw pixel data. This raw data is then re-encoded into the JPEG format. WebP's compression algorithms are more advanced, meaning it may store different information about colors, transparency, and image structure than JPEG does. During conversion, some details (especially transparency) may be lost, since JPEG doesn't support alpha channels like WebP.
