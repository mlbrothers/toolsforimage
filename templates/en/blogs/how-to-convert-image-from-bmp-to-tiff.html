Introduction

When it comes to working with digital images, understanding different file formats is crucial, especially when handling formats like BMP and TIFF. BMP, short for Bitmap, is a widely used format for storing digital images, particularly on Windows systems. BMP meaning is simple—an uncompressed, large file format that retains high-quality images but can take up significant space. On the other hand, TIFF, or Tagged Image File Format, is known for its flexibility in handling large images and is often used in professional environments, such as in graphic design and photography. TIFF meaning revolves around a format that supports multiple layers, making it ideal for high-quality editing and printing tasks.
