TIFF to JPEG Conversion Guide

Published on: October 20, 2024

Introduction

TIFF and JPEG are two of the most widely used image formats, but they serve different purposes. TIFF is known for retaining high-quality details, making it a preferred format for professional photographers and designers. On the other hand, JPEG is a compressed format that is more suitable for web use, sharing, and saving storage space. If you find yourself needing to convert TIFF to JPEG, this guide will walk you through the process.

Before diving into how to convert TIFF to JPEG, it’s essential to understand why you might need to convert between the two formats. TIFF files are often large due to their lossless compression, meaning no quality is lost in the image. JPEG files, however, are compressed, allowing for faster loading times and smaller file sizes. This makes JPEG more convenient for online use, while TIFF is often used for printing or archiving images in their highest quality.
