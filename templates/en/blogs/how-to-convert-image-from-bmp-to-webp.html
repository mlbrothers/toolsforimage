Introduction

When working with image formats, understanding the differences between BMP and WEBP is essential. BMP, or Bitmap Image File, is a format that has been around for a long time. It's known for its simplicity and uncompressed image quality, making it suitable for high-quality image storage. However, due to its large file size, BMP isn't ideal for web usage. That's where WEBP comes into play.

WEBP is a modern image format developed by Google. It offers both lossless and lossy compression, meaning that you can maintain a good balance between image quality and file size. WEBP is highly efficient, especially for websites, as it loads faster without sacrificing image clarity. If you're looking to reduce your website's loading time, converting BMP to WEBP is a smart move.
